at a glance

for clients, GTrends is an easily digestible way to help clients plan their own marketing campaigns after DTP ends.

for Leadflow, GTrends would be a supplementary tool that informs when to redirect attention to seasonal clients. Very poor at local SEO, phenomenal at identifying long-tail and rising keywords.

Drag your mouse across this datamap to see when these keywords peak in usage. You should notice a clear seasonal trend in September; this is especially significant given how the trend is present pre- & post-pandemic, demonstrating long-term viability of the service.

use cases

for clients...

GTrends is built to be user-friendly. even for folks with less tech proficiency, GTrends boils down to:

  1. insert search term
  2. adjust date range
  3. specify location

combined with live-updates, this is the closest a regular user can come to being "up-to-date on trends" in Google

after DTP is over, clients will still need marketing! GTrends offers a simple macro-level solution to determine when owners need to invest or divest energy from marketing efforts.

Tool can rapidly over-complicate if client doesn't know how to search for keywords, or know what they're supposed to look for.

is "yet another tool" that client may not need... e.g., what if they're not seasonal? what if their actual customer base behaves differently than GTrend data?

for Leadflow...

it's free, it's near-live Google results, and is especially helpful to analyze geographical trends in keyword interest.


ge's own belief is that with the advent of AI implemented into search engines, SEO is headed towards a long-tail query golden age of asking high-intent queries rather than singular keywords. GTrends does great at identifying phrases, not just keywords.

similar to client, knowing when to seasonally redirect energy towards certain clients to boost our KPI optimizes our time. Training time to learn GTrends is minimal; user-friendliness + clear documentation by Google makes onboarding smooth.

Another tool in our kit means clearly establishing when GTrends is the "right fit". Especially if DTP grows, we need to minimize bloat at all corners-- especially with what tools we use.

GTrends is ***terrible for local SEO.*** With an inability to specify counties/boroughs, GTrends falls especially short in NYC.
